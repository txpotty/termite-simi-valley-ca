Common Problems Indicating Termite Trouble:

  • Discovering Mud Tubes: These narrow tunnels built by subterranean termites on foundation walls or other surfaces indicate active infestation and migration.

    Mud tubes a clear sign that subterranean termites are actively moving between their underground colony and your home's wood structures. Recognizing and addressing these early can prevent significant structural damage.

  • Finding Piles of Discarded Wings: Swarming termites shed their wings after finding a new location to establish a colony, often leaving small piles near windows or light sources.

    Finding discarded wings is a tell-tale sign of a recent termite swarm, indicating that a new colony may be attempting to establish itself near or within your property. Prompt action is essential to prevent infestation.

  • Hearing Clicking Sounds in Walls: Soldier termites may make clicking sounds with their jaws when disturbed.

    While not always easy to detect, clicking sounds within your walls can be an auditory clue that termites are present and actively working within the wood structures. This is a sign that should be investigated further by a professional.

  • Noticing Damaged or Hollow-Sounding Wood: Termites eat wood from the inside out, leaving a thin veneer that can sound hollow when tapped.

    Wood that sounds hollow when tapped or appears damaged could be a sign of termites eating away at the interior. This internal damage can be extensive before it becomes outwardly visible, highlighting the importance of early detection.

  • Observing Bubbling or Peeling Paint/Wallpaper: Termite activity and the moisture they can bring can cause paint or wallpaper to bubble or peel.

    Unexplained bubbling or peeling of paint or wallpaper can be an indirect indicator of termite activity or moisture issues that attract termites. Investigating these visual anomalies can lead to the discovery of a hidden infestation.
